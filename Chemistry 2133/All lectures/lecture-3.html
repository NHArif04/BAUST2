Quantum Number

Question:

What is Quantum Number? Describe them.

The number which used to explain the complete arrangement of electrons around the nucleus of atom are know as quantum number. There are
  1. Principal quantum number (n)
  2. Azimuthal quantum number (l) → Angular momentum Q.N.
  3. Magnetic quantum number (m)
  4. Spin quantum number (s)

Principal Quantum Number:

The quantum number which used to explain the distance of an electron from the nucleus of an atom is known as principal quantum number. Denoted by n = 1,2,3,... any integer number. If the value of n increase the distance of electron from the nucleus will also increase.

Azimuthal Quantum Number: Important

The quantum number which used to explain the number and name of sub-shell present in any shell is known as azimuthal quantum number. Denoted by l and its value is from 0 to n-1 (integer value).
:. l = 0 → (n-1)
When l = 0, sub-shell will be 's'
When l = 1, sub-shell will be 'p'
When l = 2, sub-shell will be 'd'
When l = 3, sub-shell will be 'f'

Magnetic Quantum Number: Important

The quantum number which used to explain the number of orbital present in any shell or sub-shell is known as magnetic quantum number. Denoted by m.
:. m = +l → 0 → -l
The number of m indicate number of orbital.

Spin Quantum Number:

The quantum number which used to explain the rotation of two electrons in an orbital is known as spin quantum number. Denoted by s.
:. s = +1/2, -1/2
